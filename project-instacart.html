The Business Challenge

Instacart's marketing and sales teams needed to understand:

  • What are the busiest days and times for orders to optimize ad scheduling?
  • When do customers spend the most money to target promotional campaigns?
  • Which product departments have the highest frequency of orders?
  • How do customer demographics (age, income, family status) influence purchasing behavior?
  • What are the differences in ordering habits among different customer profiles?
Key Question: How can Instacart use customer data to create targeted marketing campaigns that increase customer engagement and drive sales across different demographic segments?

My Approach

1. Data Collection & Integration

The project involved working with multiple large datasets that needed to be carefully integrated:

  • Orders Data: 3+ million order records with timestamps and customer IDs
  • Products Data: Product information across 21 departments
  • Customer Data: Demographic information including age, income, region, and family status
  • Order Products Data: Detailed line-item data connecting orders to specific products

Key Findings & Insights

Finding #1: Peak Shopping Times Orders peaked between 10am-4pm on weekends, with Saturday and Sunday showing 30% higher order volumes than weekdays. This identified optimal times for promotional campaigns and surge pricing strategies.
Finding #2: Loyalty Patterns Customers with dependents showed 45% higher reorder rates and spent 25% more per order than single customers, indicating a valuable segment for loyalty program targeting.
Finding #3: Department Preferences Produce, dairy, and snacks were the top three departments across all segments, but high-income customers showed significantly higher spending in specialty and organic departments.
Finding #4: Age-Based Behavior Customers aged 35-45 represented the highest value segment, with the largest basket sizes and most frequent ordering patterns, suggesting they should be the primary target for retention campaigns.
Finding #5: Regional Variations Geographic analysis revealed significant regional differences in product preferences and ordering times, indicating the need for localized marketing strategies.

Challenges & Solutions

Challenge 1: Large Dataset Performance

Problem: Working with 3+ million records caused memory issues and slow processing times in Python.

Solution: Implemented chunked data processing, optimized pandas operations using vectorization, and strategically filtered data to reduce memory footprint by 60%. Used data types optimization (e.g., category dtype for categorical variables).

Challenge 2: Missing Data

Problem: Approximately 10% of customer records had missing demographic information.

Solution: Conducted thorough missing data analysis to understand patterns. For critical variables, created an "Unknown" category rather than dropping records. Documented all assumptions and validated results with sensitivity analysis.

Challenge 3: Data Integration Complexity

Problem: Multiple datasets with different granularities and potential key mismatches.

Solution: Carefully validated merge operations at each step, checking for unexpected duplicates or data loss. Created detailed documentation of merge logic and performed QA checks on record counts.

Challenge 4: Customer Segmentation Overlap

Problem: Some customers fell into multiple behavioral segments, making clean categorization difficult.

Solution: Created hierarchical segmentation logic with clear prioritization rules. Documented overlap cases and their business implications in the final recommendations.
